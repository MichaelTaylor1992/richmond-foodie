RICHMOND RESTAURANT NEWS

Stony Point Fashion Park to be site of new cheesecake bistro

Local cheesecake baker Cheryl Wittman, owner of Say Cheese…Cake, is opening Verseau Bistro in Stony Point Fashion Park. The bistro will offer a range of cheesecakes, coffee, wine, beer and charcuterie at the bistro. It will be located across from Vineyard Vines near P. F. Chang’s. It has a planned early December opening

New York Deli Owner takes over Lady N’Awlins Café

The ownership group of New York Deli has taken ownership of the building of the former Lady N’Awlins Café at 2329 W. Main Street in Richmond. However, instead of running the restaurant themselves, they will be turning it over to pair of Deli employees, Jacque Branch and Rickee Jones. Although they are still finalizing the menu, it will be focused on the bayou. An early 2023 opening is planned.

Midlothian’s Brick House Diner Taking over Kitchen 64 Space

With the closing of the popular Kitchen 64, the owners of Brick House Diner on Midlothian Turnpike have decided to expand and will open in the space located at 3336 N. Arthur Ashe Boulevard. Initially, Boulevard Brick House will be open for breakfast and lunch, but hope to expand to include dinner later. In addition to the Boulevard location, the Midlothian location recently has signed a contract to move from its current location in 2024 to Westchester Commons.

Tazza Kitchen co-founder to bring Boston-based Burtons Grill & Bar to Carytown

John Haggai, co-fonder of Tazza Kitchen, is returning to Richmond with the first Richmond location of Boston-based Burtons Grill & Bar. It is designed to be similar to high-end steak and seafood restaurants but in a more casual atmosphere. It will be located in the new Carytown Exchange Shopping.
